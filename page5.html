Ethical Use of Generative AI

The responsible, fair, and honest use of generative AI.

Ethical use of generative AI means using AI tools responsibly, honestly, and safely. AI should be used to support learning and creativity, not to mislead, cheat, or cause harm. Humans must always stay in control of how AI is used.

AI-generated content should not be presented as entirely one’s own work. Users must be honest about using AI and take responsibility for the final outcome. AI does not think or judge, so humans must decide what is right or wrong.

AI can sometimes give incorrect or misleading information. Ethical use requires users to think critically, verify facts, and not blindly trust AI outputs, especially for important tasks.

Ethical use of AI also involves being aware of bias, protecting privacy, and using AI for positive purposes. Users should avoid promoting stereotypes, sharing personal or confidential information, or using AI to deceive or harm others. AI should be used to support learning, creativity, and efficiency, not replace genuine effort.
